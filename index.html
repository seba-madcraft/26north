26 North Yachts

Strategic Paid Media Pivot Plan & Architecture Restructure

The Current Landscape: Why We Must Pivot

To scale 26 North Yachts' lead generation effectively, we must address three critical bottlenecks limiting our growth and frustrating the sales team.

Problem 1: The Tracking Black Hole
Currently, campaigns are generating leads, but the data is being lost in transit. Non-native forms and third-party webhooks (Make.com) are stripping essential tracking IDs (GCLID/Meta Click ID) before they reach Zoho. Google and Meta are optimizing blind.
Problem 2: The "Junk Lead" Epidemic
Sales efficiency is being drained by low-intent leads, bots, and "syndicated search" traffic. Without Offline Conversion Tracking (OCT) to tell the algorithm which leads are actually responsive, the platforms continue to buy cheap, unqualified traffic.
Problem 3: Budget Fragmentation & Missed Opportunities
A $1,400 monthly budget is being allocated to Bing with volatile results, while our primary Google campaigns are "Limited by Budget". Concurrently, competitors like Burgess and Denison dominate bottom-funnel Search. We need a proactive strategy to bypass the Search auction and create demand.

The Solution

This document outlines a 3-phase strategic pivot to fix the data architecture, reallocate wasted spend into proactive Demand Generation, and harden the funnel against junk traffic.

Phase 1: The "Signal-to-Scale" Framework

Objective: Rebuild the tracking infrastructure to stop data loss and enable Offline Conversion Tracking (OCT) via API.

The Core Argument: Machine learning algorithms are only as good as the data they receive. By making the Zoho Pipeline the "brain" of our campaigns, we teach Google and Meta to bid only on users who mirror the behavior of responsive, high-net-worth clients.

Execution Steps:

  • Form Standardization: Simplify the architecture. Migrate to native Zoho forms to ensure seamless data flow and eliminate Make.com points of failure.
  • GCLID Capture: Create hidden fields to capture and pass tracking IDs directly into Zoho pipeline records.
  • API Value Sync: Configure the Zoho-to-Google API to fire conversion events only when a lead status changes to "Responsive" or "Qualified".

Phase 2: The "Broker Proxy" Demand Gen Strategy

Objective: Overcome the "broker-to-broker" limitation and attack top-tier market share on the East Coast.

The Opportunity: By reallocating the $1,400 monthly Bing budget directly into Google Demand Gen, we bypass the saturated Search auction. If buyers use brokers, we target the brokers.

Execution Steps:

  • Budget Reallocation: Pause the underperforming Bing Ads account. Shift the $1,400/month budget to Google Demand Gen campaigns to maximize Google's AI capabilities.
  • Seed Audience Refinement (1st Party Data): Since "Closed Sales" lack the volume needed for machine learning, we will extract a 1st party data list of "Responsive Leads" from Zoho. This bypasses the "ghosting" issue.
  • Lookalike (LAL) Deployment: Use this high-quality seed list to generate LALs for Demand Gen, targeting the East Coast market with high-end, lifestyle-focused video and image assets.

Phase 3: Full-Funnel Consolidation & Hygiene

Objective: Maximize efficiency by eliminating budget constraints and protecting the sales team from "junk" leads.

The Defense Strategy: We must harden the funnel before scaling budget. A smaller volume of highly qualified leads is infinitely more profitable than a high volume of unverified form fills.

Execution Steps:

  • Remove "Limited by Budget" Constraints: With Bing paused and form tracking fixed, consolidate Google Search campaigns. Implement Portfolio Bidding targeting only refined, high-intent keywords.
  • Lead Form Friction (Meta): Implement 2FA (Phone/SMS Verification) on Meta Instant Forms. This adds necessary friction to filter out accidental clicks, kids, and bots.
  • Aggressive Negative Keyword Expansion: Maintain aggressive negative keyword sweeps (e.g., "cheap", "jobs", "salary", "how to") to shield the new AI-driven Search Max campaigns from irrelevant traffic.
