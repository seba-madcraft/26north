Strategic Paid Media Pivot Plan (Google & Meta)

Phase 1: The "Signal-to-Scale" Framework

Objective: Rebuild the tracking infrastructure to stop data loss and enable Offline Conversion Tracking (OCT) via API.

The Core Issue: Current lead forms are not native to Zoho, requiring fragile third-party webhooks (Make.com) that strip critical marketing data before it reaches the CRM.

Execution Steps:

  • Form Standardization: Simplify the form architecture. Migrate to native Zoho forms (or direct API integrations) embedded on the site to ensure seamless data flow.
  • GCLID Capture: Create hidden fields in the new forms to capture and pass the GCLID (Google Click ID) and Meta Click IDs directly into Zoho pipeline records.
  • API Value Sync: Configure the Zoho-to-Google API to fire conversion events back to Google Ads only when a lead status changes to "Responsive" or "Qualified", training the algorithm on actual business value rather than raw form submissions.

Phase 2: The "Broker Proxy" Demand Gen Strategy

Objective: Overcome the "broker-to-broker" limitation and attack top-tier market share on the East Coast.

The Opportunity: Market giants like Burgess and Denison dominate Search Volume (5,400 and 1,600 SV respectively). By reallocating the $1,400 monthly Bing budget directly into Google Demand Gen, we can bypass the saturated Search auction and build luxury brand authority visually.

Execution Steps:

  • Budget Reallocation: Pause the underperforming Bing Ads account entirely. Shift the $1,400/month budget to Google Demand Gen campaigns.
  • Seed Audience Refinement (1st Party Data): Since "Closed Sales" lack the volume needed for machine learning, we will extract a 1st party data list of "Responsive Leads" from Zoho. This bypasses the "ghosting" issue and trains the algorithm to find users who actively engage[cite: 805, 899].
  • Lookalike (LAL) Deployment: Use this high-quality seed list to generate LALs for Demand Gen, targeting the East Coast market with high-end, lifestyle-focused video and image assets[cite: 2631, 2646].

Phase 3: Full-Funnel Consolidation & Hygiene

Objective: Maximize efficiency by eliminating budget constraints and protecting the sales team from "junk" leads.

The Defense Strategy: Historical data shows a severe issue with bots, "syndicated search", and unqualified leads draining sales resources[cite: 192, 850]. We must harden the funnel before scaling budget.

Execution Steps:

  • Remove "Limited by Budget" Constraints: With Bing paused and form tracking fixed, consolidate Google Search campaigns. Implement Portfolio Bidding (Maximize Conversions) targeting only the refined, high-intent keywords.
  • Lead Form Friction (Meta): Implement 2FA (Phone Number Verification) on Meta Instant Forms. This adds necessary friction to filter out accidental clicks and bots, ensuring the sales team only receives high-intent inquiries.
  • Continuous Negative Keyword Expansion: Maintain aggressive negative keyword sweeps to exclude non-commercial traffic (e.g., "cheap", "jobs", "salary", "how to") from triggering the new AI-driven Search Max campaigns.
